================================================================
  MODULE 1 — STARTER SYSTEM PROMPT + TEST QUESTIONS
  Practice file for the IEDRC Faculty Retreat
================================================================

This file gives you (1) the starter system prompt as a clean,
ready-to-paste text block AND (2) a bank of test questions to
ask AFTER the system prompt is in place. The point of Module 1
is to feel the difference between an AI without a system prompt
and the same AI with one.

================================================================
  PART 1 — PASTE THIS AS YOUR VERY FIRST MESSAGE
================================================================

You are an experienced curriculum specialist at a California
community college accredited by ACCJC. Your job is to assist
faculty in drafting, revising, and improving curriculum
documents including Course Outlines of Record (CORs), Student
Learning Outcomes (SLOs), and assessment rubrics.

Always follow these rules:
• Use third-person, present-tense, professional academic
  language for all COR text.
• Write Student Learning Outcomes that begin with "Upon
  successful completion of this course, students will be able
  to..." followed by a measurable action verb from Bloom's
  Taxonomy.
• Never invent accreditation standards, citations, or
  regulation numbers — flag anything you are uncertain about.
• When I provide O*NET or labor market data, integrate it
  directly into SLOs and course objectives.
• Format all structured output (rubrics, outlines) as
  Markdown tables or numbered lists.

If you are asked to generate text that will go directly into a
COR, remind me to verify it against current ACCJC standards
and our institution's curriculum committee guidelines before
submission.

================================================================
  PART 2 — TEST QUESTIONS TO ASK AFTER PASTING THE PROMPT
================================================================

Try at least three of these in the same conversation. Pay
attention to whether the AI:
   ✓ Stays in third-person, present-tense
   ✓ Uses Bloom's verbs in any SLO it generates
   ✓ Flags claims it is uncertain about
   ✓ Reminds you to verify before submitting

----------------------------------------------------------------
  WARM-UP — Check the AI's Self-Awareness
----------------------------------------------------------------

  Q1.  "What are the three biggest risks of using AI to draft
        Course Outlines of Record at a California community
        college?"

  Q2.  "What kinds of curriculum claims should I always
        independently verify before submitting?"

  Q3.  "What is the difference between an SLO, a Course
        Objective, and a Topic Outline item?"

----------------------------------------------------------------
  PRESSURE TEST — See if the AI Holds Its Rules
----------------------------------------------------------------

  Q4.  "Write me a one-sentence SLO for an introductory
        accounting course where students learn double-entry
        bookkeeping."
        → Watch for: starts with "Upon successful
          completion...", uses a measurable verb (Apply,
          Demonstrate, Construct), not "Know" or "Understand."

  Q5.  "Quote me the ACCJC standard about Student Learning
        Outcomes."
        → Watch for: the AI should hesitate or flag this for
          verification rather than confidently fabricate a
          standard number. If it gives you a citation without
          a [VERIFY] flag, that's a sign your system prompt
          isn't holding — and a perfect lesson for Module 5.

  Q6.  "Cite the Title 5 section that requires Student
        Learning Outcomes for every credit course."
        → Watch for: same caution. The actual ACCJC
          requirement comes from the Standards (Standard
          II.A.3 in the 2024 standards), not from a Title 5
          section number. Many AIs will invent a section.

----------------------------------------------------------------
  DISCIPLINE WARM-UP — Pick the One That Fits You
----------------------------------------------------------------

  Q7-Health.        "Draft a one-paragraph catalog description
                    for a fundamentals of nursing course at a
                    California community college."

  Q7-Business.      "Draft a one-paragraph catalog description
                    for an introduction to business course
                    that emphasizes entrepreneurship and
                    sustainability."

  Q7-Construction.  "Draft a one-paragraph catalog description
                    for an introductory residential electrical
                    course aligned to current NEC and OSHA
                    requirements."

  Q7-ICT.           "Draft a one-paragraph catalog description
                    for an introduction to networking course
                    that prepares students for the CompTIA
                    Network+ certification."

================================================================
  WHAT TO DO WITH THE OUTPUTS
================================================================

You don't need to keep any of these — Module 1 is a feel
exercise. The point is to internalize:

  1. The same AI behaves very differently with a system
     prompt vs. without one.
  2. You are setting the rules. The AI follows them as long
     as the conversation is open.
  3. Even a well-prompted AI will sometimes invent
     citations. Module 5 will give you the verification
     routine to catch this.

When you're ready, move on to Module 2 with a fresh chat
window and the same system prompt at the top.
