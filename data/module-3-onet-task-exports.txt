================================================================
  1. HEALTH — Registered Nurses
================================================================

SOC Code:                  29-1141
O*NET Code:                29-1141.00
Bright Outlook:            Yes (Rapid Growth + Numerous Openings)
Median Hourly Wage (US):   $42.80
Median Annual Wage (US):   $89,010
California Median Annual:  $137,690
Typical Education:         Bachelor's degree (BSN) or
                           Associate's degree (ADN)

CORE TASKS (paste these into the Module 3 prompt):

• Maintain accurate, detailed reports and records of patient
  care, including medications administered, treatments
  performed, and patient responses.
• Monitor, record, and report symptoms or changes in patients'
  conditions to the healthcare team.
• Record patients' medical information and vital signs in
  electronic health record systems.
• Modify patient treatment plans as indicated by patients'
  responses and conditions, in collaboration with the care
  team.
• Consult and coordinate with healthcare team members to
  assess, plan, implement, or evaluate patient care plans.
• Order, interpret, and evaluate diagnostic tests to identify
  and assess patients' conditions.
• Monitor all aspects of patient care, including diet and
  physical activity.
• Direct or supervise less-skilled nursing or healthcare
  personnel, or supervise a particular unit.
• Prepare patients for, and assist with, examinations or
  treatments.
• Administer medications to patients and monitor patients
  for reactions or side effects.
• Provide health care, first aid, immunizations, or
  assistance in convalescence or rehabilitation.
• Instruct individuals, families, or other groups on topics
  such as health education, disease prevention, or childbirth,
  and develop health improvement programs.
• Perform physical examinations, make tentative diagnoses,
  and treat patients en route to hospitals or in emergency
  situations.
• Conduct specified laboratory tests.
• Hand items to surgeons during operations.

================================================================
  2. BUSINESS — General and Operations Managers
================================================================

SOC Code:                  11-1021
O*NET Code:                11-1021.00
Bright Outlook:            Yes (Numerous Openings)
Median Hourly Wage (US):   $48.45
Median Annual Wage (US):   $100,780
California Median Annual:  $116,470
Typical Education:         Bachelor's degree (some positions
                           accept Associate's + experience)

CORE TASKS (paste these into the Module 3 prompt):

• Direct and coordinate activities of businesses or
  departments concerned with the production, pricing, sales,
  or distribution of products or services.
• Manage staff, prepare work schedules, and assign specific
  duties to employees.
• Review financial statements, sales and activity reports,
  and other performance data to measure productivity and goal
  achievement, and to determine areas needing cost reduction
  and program improvement.
• Establish and implement departmental policies, goals,
  objectives, and procedures, conferring with board members,
  organization officials, and staff members as necessary.
• Direct and coordinate the organization's financial and
  budget activities to fund operations, maximize investments,
  and increase efficiency.
• Determine staffing requirements, and interview, hire, and
  train new employees, or oversee those personnel processes.
• Monitor businesses and agencies to ensure that they
  efficiently and effectively provide needed services while
  staying within budgetary limits.
• Locate, select, and procure merchandise for resale,
  representing management in purchase negotiations.
• Plan and direct activities such as sales promotions,
  coordinating with other department heads as required.
• Implement or oversee environmental management or
  sustainability programs.
• Develop or implement product-marketing strategies, including
  advertising campaigns or sales promotions.
• Negotiate or approve contracts or agreements with suppliers,
  distributors, federal or state agencies, or other
  organizational entities.
• Recommend locations for new facilities, or oversee the
  remodeling or renovating of current facilities.
• Develop and maintain relationships with key suppliers and
  customer groups.

  3. CONSTRUCTION — Electricians
================================================================

SOC Code:                  47-2111
O*NET Code:                47-2111.00
Bright Outlook:            Yes (Rapid Growth)
Median Hourly Wage (US):   $30.46
Median Annual Wage (US):   $63,310
California Median Annual:  $79,700
Typical Education:         High school diploma + apprenticeship
                           (4-5 years), then state licensure

CORE TASKS (paste these into the Module 3 prompt):

• Use a variety of tools and equipment, such as power
  construction equipment, measuring devices, power tools, and
  testing equipment, including digital multimeters,
  oscilloscopes, ammeters, and test lamps.
• Inspect electrical systems, equipment, or components to
  identify hazards, defects, or the need for adjustment or
  repair, and to ensure compliance with the National
  Electrical Code (NEC) and local codes.
• Test electrical systems or continuity of circuits in
  electrical wiring, equipment, or fixtures, using testing
  devices to ensure compatibility and safety of the system.
• Connect wires to circuit breakers, transformers, or other
  components.
• Plan layout and installation of electrical wiring,
  equipment, or fixtures, based on job specifications and
  current local and national electrical codes.
• Diagnose malfunctioning systems, apparatus, or components,
  using test equipment and hand tools to locate the cause of
  a breakdown and correct the problem.
• Maintain a current electrician's license or identification
  card to meet governmental regulations.
• Repair or replace wiring, equipment, or fixtures, using
  hand tools or power tools.
• Place conduit (pipes or tubing) inside designated
  partitions, walls, or other concealed areas, and pull
  insulated wires or cables through the conduit to complete
  circuits.
• Prepare sketches showing the location of wiring or
  equipment, or follow diagrams or blueprints to ensure that
  wiring conforms to specifications and codes.
• Install ground leads and connect power cables to equipment.
• Perform business-management duties such as maintaining
  records and files, preparing reports, or ordering supplies
  and equipment.
• Provide preliminary sketches or cost estimates for
  materials or services.
• Install electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure,
  battery storage systems, and renewable-energy interconnects
  (solar PV) to current code.
• Work from ladders, scaffolds, or roofs to install,
  maintain, or repair electrical wiring, equipment, or
  fixtures.

================================================================
  4. ICT — Network and Computer Systems Administrators
================================================================

SOC Code:                  15-1244
O*NET Code:                15-1244.00
Bright Outlook:            Yes (Numerous Openings)
Median Hourly Wage (US):   $46.13
Median Annual Wage (US):   $95,950
California Median Annual:  $114,720
Typical Education:         Bachelor's degree common; many
                           employers accept Associate's plus
                           industry certifications (CCNA,
                           Network+, Security+, AWS Cloud
                           Practitioner)

CORE TASKS (paste these into the Module 3 prompt):

• Diagnose, troubleshoot, and resolve hardware, software, or
  other network and system problems, and replace defective
  components when necessary.
• Maintain and administer computer networks and related
  computing environments, including computer hardware,
  systems software, applications software, and all
  configurations.
• Plan, coordinate, and implement network security measures
  to protect data, software, and hardware.
• Configure, monitor, and maintain email applications or
  virus protection software.
• Operate master consoles to monitor the performance of
  computer systems and networks and to coordinate computer
  network access and use.
• Design, configure, and test computer hardware, networking
  software, and operating system software.
• Monitor network performance to determine whether
  adjustments need to be made.
• Confer with network users about how to solve existing
  system problems.
• Recommend changes to improve systems and network
  configurations, and determine hardware or software
  requirements related to such changes.
• Maintain logs related to network functions, as well as
  maintenance and repair records.
• Train people in computer system use.
• Perform routine network startup and shutdown procedures,
  and maintain control records.
• Implement and provide technical support for voice services
  and equipment, such as private branch exchange (PBX),
  voice mail systems, or related telecommunications
  equipment.
• Coordinate with vendors and with company personnel to
  facilitate purchases of cloud services and on-premises
  hardware.
• Configure and administer Active Directory, identity and
  access management (IAM), and single sign-on (SSO) systems.
• Implement, configure, and monitor cloud infrastructure
  (AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud), including virtual networks
  and zero-trust security controls.
