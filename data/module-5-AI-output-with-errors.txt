────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This course introduces students to the field of residential

Article 690 (2020 edition) and meets the training requirements
of Title 5, Section 55003.5 of the California Code of
Regulations governing Career Technical Education curriculum

STUDENT LEARNING OUTCOMES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Upon successful completion of this course, students will be

SLO 1.   Understand the fundamental principles of

SLO 2.   Know the components of a residential solar PV

SLO 3.   Appreciate the role of solar energy in California's

SLO 4.   Calculate string-sizing for a residential rooftop PV

SLO 5.   Demonstrate the safe installation of a residential

4. To verify that a completed PV installation passes the

              The 12 planted errors, by type

ERROR #1 — HALLUCINATED REGULATION NUMBER
  Where:  Catalog description, "Title 5, Section 55003.5"
  What's wrong:  Title 5 Section 55003 is the prerequisite
  regulation. There is no Section 55003.5 governing CTE in
  renewable energy. AI invented this section number.
  Fix:  Strike the citation entirely or replace with the
  correct regulation (Title 5 §55070 covers CTE programs).

ERROR #2 — HALLUCINATED INDUSTRY CERTIFICATION
  Where:  Catalog description, "NABCEP CertSolar"
  What's wrong:  The actual NABCEP entry-level credential is
  the "PV Associate" (formerly "Entry-Level Exam"). There is
  no certification called "CertSolar."
  Fix:  Replace with "NABCEP PV Associate Credential."

ERROR #3 — OUTDATED NEC EDITION
  Where:  Catalog description, "NEC Article 690 (2020 edition)"
  What's wrong:  The NEC is updated on a 3-year cycle. The
  2026 NEC has been adopted in California. AI's training data
  is stale.
  Fix:  Reference "current NEC Article 690 as adopted by
  California."

ERROR #4 — VAGUE BLOOM'S VERB ("Understand")
  Where:  SLO 1 — "Understand the fundamental principles..."
  What's wrong:  "Understand" is not measurable. ACCJC
  Standard II.A.3 requires assessable outcomes.
  Fix:  "Explain the fundamental principles..." or "Describe
  the photoelectric effect..."

ERROR #5 — VAGUE BLOOM'S VERB ("Know")
  Where:  SLO 2 — "Know the components..."
  Fix:  "Identify the components..." or "Describe the
  function of the components..."

ERROR #6 — VAGUE BLOOM'S VERB ("Appreciate") + UNVERIFIED
            POLICY CLAIM
  Where:  SLO 3 — "Appreciate the role of solar energy..."
  What's wrong:  "Appreciate" is non-measurable AND the
  "zero-carbon grid by 2030" claim is incorrect. SB 100
  targets 100% zero-carbon retail electricity by 2045
  (interim 60% renewable by 2030). AI conflated targets.
  Fix:  Rewrite using a measurable verb and verify the
  policy citation.

ERROR #7 — UNIT / HOUR ARITHMETIC ERROR
  Where:  Header — "3.0 units / 36 hours lecture + 72 hours
  laboratory (108 total contact hours)"
  What's wrong:  Per Title 5 §55002.5 contact-hour formulas,
  3 units does not match 36+72 hours. A standard 3-unit
  lecture-lab course is closer to 54 lecture + 54 lab (108
  total) for equal weighting OR 36 lecture + 108 lab for
  ASCCC heavier-lab guidance. The combination "36+72=108"
  is closer to a 4-unit course under common formulas.
  Fix:  Recalculate units to match hours per your local
  unit-hour formula (commonly 18:54 for lecture and 36:54
  for lab) or change the hours to match 3 units.

ERROR #8 — PREREQUISITE STATED INFORMALLY
  Where:  Header — "Students should have a basic
  understanding of electricity and be familiar with hand
  tools."
  What's wrong:  Title 5 §55003 requires prerequisites be
  stated as either (a) a specific course/competency that has
  been validated through content review, OR (b) "None."
  Loose language like "basic understanding" cannot legally
  serve as a prerequisite.
  Fix:  Either "Prerequisite: ELEC 100" (with Content
  Review Form on file) or "Prerequisite: None;
  Recommended Preparation: ELEC 100 or equivalent."

ERROR #9 — INCORRECT CSU GE AREA
  Where:  Header — "CSU GE Area B-7 (Lifelong Learning)"
  What's wrong:  CSU GE Area B-7 does not exist. CSU GE Area
  B has subareas B1, B2, B3, and B4. "Lifelong Learning"
  was Area E in the old system but is being phased out under
  Cal-GETC.
  Fix:  Verify the actual articulated GE area on
  ASSIST.org and update — or strike the GE claim if the
  course is not GE-articulated.

ERROR #10 — HALLUCINATED OSHA CITATION
  Where:  SLO 5 / Course Content — "OSHA 29 CFR 1926.501
  fall-protection requirements... for work above 4 feet"
  What's wrong:  29 CFR 1926.501 is the correct fall
  protection standard for construction, BUT the threshold
  is 6 feet, not 4 feet. AI conflated the general industry
  threshold (4 feet, 29 CFR 1910.28) with construction.
  Fix:  Either correct to "6 feet on residential rooftops"
  OR cite 29 CFR 1910.28 if discussing general industry.

ERROR #11 — HALLUCINATED NEC SUBSECTION
  Where:  Course Content — "Section 690.8.B.2.a"
  What's wrong:  NEC subsection structure is letters then
  numbers, not letters then numbers then letters. The
  string "690.8(B)(2)(a)" is not a valid NEC subdivision in
  any current edition. AI invented a plausible-looking
  citation.
  Fix:  Verify the exact subsection in the current NEC
  (Section 690.8(B) covers overcurrent protection; the
  subdivisions are (1), (2), and (3) only).

ERROR #12 — HALLUCINATED TEXTBOOK / FABRICATED ISBN
  Where:  Textbook — "Smith, J. & García, M. (2023).
  Residential Solar Installation for the California Trades.
  McGraw-Hill, 4th edition. ISBN: 978-0-07-549892-1."
  What's wrong:  This book does not exist. Title, authors,
  edition, and ISBN are all fabricated — a classic AI
  hallucination. The ISBN itself is the wrong format /
  registrant prefix for a real McGraw-Hill title.
  Fix:  Use a real, current solar PV installation textbook.
  Verify the ISBN at WorldCat.org or the publisher's
  website before listing.
