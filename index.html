Module 01Everyone
AI as Your Curriculum Partner
Move from "AI generates content" to "AI is a first-draft specialist you direct." You are still the subject matter expert — the AI handles the blank-page problem.
Context-Setting Prompts
1
Open ChatGPT, Claude, or Google AI Studio. Start a new chat. Before asking anything, set the scene — this is called a system prompt.
2
Copy the starter prompt below and paste it as your very first message. This "trains" the AI's behavior for the rest of the conversation.
3
Try asking: "What are the biggest risks of using AI for curriculum development at a California community college?" — see how the AI's self-awareness shapes its answer.
4
Read the response critically. Does the AI cite specific accreditation standards correctly? If not — that's the lesson. AI knows patterns; you know your institution.
Starter System Prompt 
You are an experienced curriculum specialist at a California community college accredited by ACCJC. Your job is to assist faculty in drafting, revising, and improving curriculum documents including Course Outlines of Record (CORs), Student Learning Outcomes (SLOs), and assessment rubrics.

Always follow these rules:
• Use third-person, present-tense, professional academic language for all COR text.
• Write Student Learning Outcomes that begin with "Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to..." followed by a measurable action verb from Bloom's Taxonomy.
• Never invent accreditation standards, citations, or regulation numbers — flag anything you are uncertain about.
• When I provide O*NET or labor market data, integrate it directly into SLOs and course objectives.
• Format all structured output (rubrics, outlines) as Markdown tables or numbered lists.

If you are asked to generate text that will go directly into a COR, remind me to verify it against current ACCJC standards and our institution's curriculum committee guidelines before submission.
Module 02Faculty
Course Outlines of Record (COR)
Draft a new catalog description, refresh an outdated course objective list, or align course content to current discipline standards — in one conversation.
Document-in / Draft-out Workflow
1
Start with the system prompt from Module 1 (or just paste it at the top of a new chat).
2
Copy your current catalog description (from CurricUNET or your syllabus) and paste it into the chat, then paste the prompt below.
3
Review the AI draft. It will suggest updated language. Ask follow-ups: "Make it more concise" or "Add a reference to current industry standards in [your field]."
4
For a full course objectives list, follow up: "Now generate 8 course objectives for this course using parallel grammatical structure starting with infinitive verbs (to analyze, to demonstrate, etc.)."
5
Before submitting to curriculum committee: verify all Title 5 language, prerequisite wording, and unit hours are correct. AI sometimes gets these wrong.

Module 03Faculty
SLOs Mapped to Labor Market
Use real O*NET occupation data to generate SLOs that demonstrate workforce relevance — one of the most powerful arguments you can make in a program review.
Labor Market → SLO Translation
1
Go to O*NET Online. Search for an occupation your course prepares students for (e.g., "Registered Nurse," "Electrician," "Network Administrator").
2
On the O*NET summary page, copy the Tasks section (the bullet list of job duties). Also note the SOC code and the occupation's median wage.
3
Paste the O*NET tasks into the chat along with the prompt below. The AI will generate SLOs directly connected to real job demands.
4
Review: Are the Bloom's verbs measurable? (Analyze, Demonstrate, Evaluate — good. Know, Understand, Appreciate — too vague.) Ask the AI to revise any SLO that uses vague verbs.
5
Optional: Ask for a mapping table: "Create a table with three columns: SLO | O*NET Task It Addresses | Bloom's Level." This is gold for program review documentation.
SLO Generator Prompt 
I am writing Student Learning Outcomes (SLOs) for the following course:

Course name: [COURSE NAME]
Course number: [e.g., NURS 101]
Discipline/Program: [YOUR DISCIPLINE]
Course level: [Introductory / Intermediate / Advanced]

The primary occupation this course prepares students for is:
Occupation: [OCCUPATION TITLE]
SOC Code: [e.g., 29-1141 for Registered Nurses]

Here are the O*NET tasks for this occupation:
[PASTE THE O*NET TASKS LIST HERE]

Please generate 5-6 Student Learning Outcomes that:
• Begin with: "Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to..."
• Use measurable Bloom's Taxonomy action verbs for this course level
• Connect directly to the O*NET tasks provided
• Are specific enough to be measured through a single assessment
• Meet ACCJC standards for authentic, measurable outcomes

After the SLOs, provide a mapping table:
| SLO # | SLO Text | O*NET Task | Bloom's Level |
Module 04Faculty
Assessments & Rubrics
Design an authentic assessment task and a complete 4-level rubric in minutes. The AI builds the structure — you bring the discipline-specific expertise to validate it.
SLO-Aligned Rubric Generation
1
Have at least one SLO ready (from Module 3 or your existing course documents). The clearer the SLO, the better the rubric.
2
Decide on an assessment type: written case study, hands-on demonstration, portfolio, group project, oral presentation, lab report — the type shapes the rubric criteria.
3
Paste the prompt below. The AI will generate both the assessment task description (what students are asked to do) and the complete rubric in one output.
4
Refine: Ask the AI to "Add a criterion for professional communication standards" or "Make the Exemplary column more specific for [YOUR FIELD]."
5
Bonus: Ask for a student-facing version of the rubric: "Rewrite this rubric in plain language that a first-semester student can understand and use to self-assess."
Rubric Generator Prompt 
Create an authentic performance assessment and a 4-level analytic rubric for:

Course: [COURSE NAME]
SLO being assessed: "[PASTE YOUR SLO TEXT HERE]"
Assessment type: [portfolio / demonstration / case study / oral presentation]
Student level: [First semester / Second year / Transfer-prep]
Context: [Brief description of program and career pathway]

Deliverables needed:

1. ASSESSMENT TASK DESCRIPTION (3-5 sentences)
Describe the task students will complete and what they must submit.

2. ANALYTIC RUBRIC (Markdown table)
• 4-5 criteria rows that directly measure the SLO
• 4 performance levels: Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1)
• Each cell: observable, specific student behaviors
• Include a professional/workplace-readiness row
• Show total possible score

3. ALIGNMENT NOTE
For each criterion, identify which part of the SLO it measures.
Module 05Everyone
Verify Before You Submit
AI hallucinations in curriculum documents can cause real accreditation problems. Learn the four-step verification routine before any AI-assisted COR goes to your curriculum committee.
AI-Assisted Quality Check
1
Step 1 — Ask for a self-audit. After the AI produces any COR text, immediately follow up with: "Review your response. List any specific claims, regulation numbers, or standards you cited that I should independently verify before submitting to a curriculum committee."
2
Step 2 — Check Bloom's verbs. Copy any SLOs and paste this prompt: "Review these SLOs. Flag any that use non-measurable verbs (understand, know, appreciate, be aware of) and suggest replacement action verbs."
3
Step 3 — Cross-check CurricUNET. Open your institution's existing COR in CurricUNET. Compare AI-suggested language to current catalog language. Verify unit values, hours, and prerequisite wording match Title 5 requirements.
4
Step 4 — Verify labor market citations. If the AI references specific employment projections or wage data, verify them directly on O*NET or CA LMI. AI training data may be outdated.
Self-Audit Prompt (run after any AI output) 
Please review the curriculum text you just generated and perform a quality audit:

1. ACCURACY CHECK
   List any regulation numbers, code citations, or accreditation language
   you referenced. Mark each as [VERIFY].

2. MEASURABILITY CHECK
   Review each SLO. Flag any with vague verbs (understand, know, appreciate).
   Suggest a replacement Bloom's Taxonomy verb for each.

3. COMPLETENESS CHECK
   Is anything missing that a complete COR section would typically include?

4. CURRENCY CHECK
   Flag any content that may be out of date — areas I should verify
   against current industry standards or state regulations.

Format as a checklist with ✓ or ⚠ for each item.

Critical Thinking Required

What to Watch For

AI is a powerful first-draft tool — but curriculum documents have real accreditation consequences. These are the three areas where AI most commonly fails faculty.

🔴
Hallucinated Facts
AI fabricates specific details with complete confidence. In curriculum work, this means invented regulation numbers, wrong code editions, non-existent certifications, and made-up labor market statistics.
  • Cites "Title 5, Section 55002.b" — always verify exact section numbers
  • References "ACCJC Standard II.A.6" — look it up yourself before submitting
  • Quotes employment growth rates it invented — only use numbers you pulled from O*NET or BLS directly
  • Mentions a "CalOSHA 1926 standard" — OSHA numbering can be mixed up between Federal/CA
  • Invents a certification (e.g., "CompTIA CyberCore") that doesn't exist
Rule: Any number, code, standard citation, or statistic in AI output must be independently verified before it appears in a curriculum document.
🟡
Accreditation Language Gaps
AI knows general academic writing conventions but does not know your institution's specific COR format requirements, your curriculum committee's preferences, or current ACCJC guidance revisions.
  • AI may write course objectives in a format your curriculum committee will reject — check your template
  • SLO wording may not match your college's local COR style guide
  • Prerequisite wording must follow Title 5 §55003 exactly — AI often paraphrases incorrectly
  • Transfer articulation language has specific CSU/UC formatting requirements that AI ignores
  • Unit value and hours calculations (lecture/lab ratios) must follow Title 5 — AI doesn't know your course's contact hour formula
Always compare: Paste AI output side-by-side with an approved COR in CurricUNET from the same course family before sending to committee.
🔵
How to Verify — The 4-Step Check
Build this four-step routine into every AI-assisted curriculum session. It takes 10–15 minutes and catches the problems that cause committee rejections and accreditation flags.
  • Run the AI self-audit prompt from Module 5 — the AI will flag its own uncertain claims
  • Check all Bloom's verbs — replace "understand," "know," and "appreciate" with measurable verbs
  • Open CurricUNET — compare structure, formatting, and prerequisite language to an approved peer COR
  • Verify every number — confirm labor market data at O*NET, BLS, or CA LMI before it enters any document
  • Get a colleague to read it — in 5 minutes, an experienced colleague will catch what you and the AI both missed
Bottom line: AI saves you the blank page. You save the document from errors. The curriculum committee approves the finished product — not the AI's draft.
